What you can do if the section 13 notice is valid:

If the notice provided is valid, then your landlord has correctly proposed the rent increase, however you still have options available to you.

You are encouraged to negotiate with your landlord to reach a more reasonable rent if you feel you may have difficulty paying the new proposed rent.

If you cannot come to an agreement with your landlord and have not yet paid the increased rent, you may bring the matter before a tribunal to assess whether or not the rent increase is unreasonable.

[We can link to the reference image of the application form here]

You must send your application by post before the date of the rent increase.

Things you will need to provide the tribunal with your application and evidence that may strengthen your case:

  • The address
  • The amount of rent you currently pay
  • A copy of your section 13 notice and tenancy agreement
  • Any repairs your landlord is responsible for on you tenancy agreement - Your case will be stronger if you can provide evidence of your landlord not managing repairs that they should be
  • Any improvements you have made to the property that you have paid for yourself
  • Find out what people tend to pay for properties like yours in your local area. If others in your area are paying an amount similar to your landlord’s proposed increase, it is likely that the tribunal will see it as a fair increase. You can use our tools for comparing property values to help calculate the average value of properties like yours in your area.

You don’t need to send your evidence with your application form - the tribunal will write to inform you of when it needs to be sent. You’ll usually have to provide evidence seven days before your hearing but keep checking your mail. The tribunal should write to you once within seven days of receiving your application and again within 28 days.

It can take up to 10 days for a tribunal to make a decision, so it is advisable to save up in case they do not agree that the rent increase is unfair.

Remember to sign and date your application form or it won’t be valid.
